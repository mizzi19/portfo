Commercial pilot

My career in aviation started at AirMalta after an ab-initio selection process where 10 of us were selected to go to Spain to BAe Flight training Europe. It was October 2001 that we proceeded to Jerez to do our ATPL. Our next step was in 2003 at Airbus (Toulouse) where wer we got our A320 type rating.
Throughout my 17 year at AirMalta I was able to operate (mainly) in Europe with interesting leases around the world such as South America, Middle East and North Africa.
Being a pilot is not only the ability of flying an aircraft in good or bad weather. That is a skill which to a certain extent is mastered in the beginning of the career and thereof checked in a simulator every 6 months throughout the career. The interesting part of this job is the soft skills that one has to acquire throughout the career and along as one matures. Being a pilot means the ability to work in a team. It aslo means that that team might change every flight you have. It is also the ability to work with people of different capabilities and personalities confined in a very limited space (an A320 flightdeck is c. 7m2).
